Bi-Cube Stool is a wood stool that helps people to meditate through sitting. It is not a comfortable stool to sit on. Instead, it gives mild pressure to the user from back to keep one sit upright. The stool acts like a platform of thinking.

BI-CUBE Hidden spline mitre joints
ITERATIONS
THE MILD PRESSUREAfter making several lo-fi models, I chose the cubic shape to move on. Then I start to consider the details and experiment with the feeling of sitting on the stool. The pressure from the back of the stool gives the user a force to the front. The horizontal face gives the user a force to the top. I have to balance a moderate length of faces and degree of the back to keep the user on the stool but also can feel the force.  The models' materials I used varies from foam core to cardboard, MDF, plywood, and their scale also started from 1/3 scale to full scale. I experiment with more different shapes with lo-fi foam core models and narrow them down to the more time-consuming models to determine details. The 1/3 plywood model is to experiment with the material and joints. Then I made larger models in order to examine the scale. Finally, I use MDF to make the functioning model to experiment with the sitting experience. Small scale modelMDF full scale model Cardboard full scale model CRAFTINGPersonal student project 20191 Month The basic form of the stool is the two geometry merging together. The two straight faces connect with each other to imitate the shape of the human's bottom. I tried different ways of combining two shapes. To stretch, to hollow, to connect, etc.  The final product is made of plywood boards. To make them look tidy, I chose the miter joint. But it is not a strong enough joint for the stool. So I added the hidden splines in the joints to add up the strength. The splines are glued in the holes drilled by the router.

The Bug Bag is a 'SPIME', a smart bag that is mainly made of recyclable waxed hemp canvas. The bag can use NFC to recognize items marked by NFC tags. When put items inside, the e-paper screen at the back of the bag can display the contents. It is like a transparent container that you can look through, also like an index that helps you to find the items.

BUG BAG

Personal student project2020EXPERIMENTS WITH WAX & CANVAS THE ELECTRONIC PART DESIGNING THE BODY OF BAG Material is the part I cut in. Hemp has a better performance in sustainability than cotton. It absorbs less water and has resistant to insects so it requires less pesticide. I chose hemp canvas to work on. To improve its performance, I tried to wax it. The waxing process gives the canvas more durability. It becomes water-resistant and hard enough to hold its shape. The shape of the bag is inspired by the shells of insects. They are hard enough to protect their body from attack, also flexible enough to enable insects to move quickly, which fits with the characteristics of the waxed canvas well. I made several paper models to experiment with the 3D shape. The bag was divided into two parts in this layout so that the user can easily access all the items. The bag needs to recognize the NFC tags and display their icons on the e-paper. I used an STM32 board, e-paper, and an NFC reader to achieve these functions. The user's own power bank is used to power the whole system so that the user can take it out to recharge and has more flexibility in using it. The boards and the screen are embedded in the back of the bag to avoid showing the item inside to strangers.  I made a mold with plaster and cover it with foil to impregnate the shell of the bag so that I can brush wax on the shell. After waxing, I used a hairdryer to blow away the extra wax to avoid the canvas getting too soft with too much wax on it.

Personal student projectTools:  Illustrator
            Html , CSS The Square Typeface

The Square type is all made from squares. I got inspiration from the Chinese characters which are also called 'Square character' in Chinese. I start to consider how would English letters look in square shape.

I made minimal change to the square base so that the whole letter still looks like a square. To do that, I only apply lines, small triangles to the square.The typewriter concept comes fromthe ancient metal character types. They are made to put into slots to form a page together. The typewriter is combining the type with a keyboard. User have to put the type into the slot in order to type, which form a interesting experience. The prototype above is made by Illustrator, while the final version is CSS and html coding.
